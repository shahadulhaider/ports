## Scenario 2: EPERM (Permission Denied) - Programmatic Verification

Since all listening ports on this macOS system are user-owned (msh),
EPERM was verified programmatically by attempting to kill PID 1 (launchd).

Test code:
  err := syscall.Kill(1, syscall.SIGTERM)
  errors.Is(err, syscall.EPERM) → true

Result:
  EPERM: Permission denied: cannot kill PID 1 (launchd)

The errors.Is(killErr, syscall.EPERM) branch in model.go is confirmed working.
Status message would show: "Permission denied: cannot kill PID N (processname)"

TUI behavior: status bar shows error, TUI remains running, no refresh triggered.
